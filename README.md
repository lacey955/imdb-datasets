# Analyzing the IMDB Top 400
Analyzing/comparing the runtimes, genres, release dates, etc. of IMDB's top 400 films.

## Questions 
- Does a movie’s length affect its rank/rating? Do longer movies tend to score better, or is shorter and tighter the way to go?
- Is there a relationship between number of votes (popularity) and rating? Do "popular" movies actually rate higher, or do smaller films sometimes score better?
- What genre shows up the most? Has this changed over time?

## Findings
- Comparing rating to runtime, no strong correlation could be found.
- We found that films with a higher number of votes tend to have higher ratings, with a slight positive relationship.
- When comparing genre releases to release years, our data may have been faulty due to prioritizing the first genre a movie was listed as alphabetically. Still, we noticed a sharp increase in the amount of acclaimed action films released in the late 20th-early 21st century.
